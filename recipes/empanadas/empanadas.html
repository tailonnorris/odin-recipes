Empanadas

Empanadas de jamon y queso


Ham and cheese empanadas are a savory variation of the traditional empanada. The pastry dough is filled with a combination of ham and cheese, typically mozzarella or a similar mild cheese. Some variations may also include chopped vegetables, such as bell peppers or onions, for added flavor and texture. The empanadas are then folded and crimped to create a half-moon shape, and baked or fried until the dough is golden and crispy. Ham and cheese empanadas are a popular snack or light meal in many Latin American countries, and can be enjoyed hot or cold.


Ingredients

  • Empanada dough (made with flour, water, salt, and sometimes eggs)
  • Ham, diced or shredded
  • Cheese, such as mozzarella, queso blanco, or a similar mild cheese
  • Chopped vegetables (optional), such as bell peppers or onions
  • Egg wash (made with beaten egg and water) or milk (for brushing the empanadas before baking)
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Prepare the filling: In a bowl, mix together the diced or shredded ham, cheese, and any optional chopped vegetables. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  2. Roll out the dough: Roll out the empanada dough to about 1/4 inch thickness, then cut out circles of dough using a cookie cutter or cup.
  3. Fill the empanadas: Place a spoonful of the filling mixture in the center of each circle of dough.
  4. Fold and crimp the edges: Fold the dough over to enclose the filling and form a half-moon shape. Use a fork to press and crimp the edges together to seal the empanadas.
  5. Brush with egg wash or milk: Brush the tops of the empanadas with egg wash or milk to create a golden and shiny crust when baked.
  6. Bake or fry the empanadas: Place the empanadas on a baking sheet and bake in the oven at 375°F (190°C) for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown. Alternatively, you can fry the empanadas in hot oil until golden and crispy.
  7. Serve: Serve the ham and cheese empanadas hot, garnished with chopped herbs or dipping sauces if desired.
